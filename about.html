The Problem

Building hi-fi systems shouldn't require a physics degree.

Matching audio components means cross-referencing impedances, output voltages, sensitivity figures, cartridge compliance, and tonearm mass across a dozen spec sheets in multiple browser tabs — for every single pairing.

One wrong pairing — a speaker impedance that dips too low for your amp, a phono gain stage that clips — and the system you spent thousands on won't reach its potential.

  • Impedance mismatches cause distortion and can damage amplifiers
  • Incorrect phono loading deadens cartridge high-frequency response
  • Poor gain staging increases the noise floor and reduces dynamics
  • Wrong tonearm resonance frequency causes audible bass coloration
The Solution
